---
title: I am Free
date: 2026-03-21
description: The day two things ended at once, and what that felt like.
---

I am starting this entry sitting in our apartment, looking down Mission St.

TWO HOURS AGO, in the middle of F45, I was checking my phone because I was waiting on a friend's response on an event we are planning, and then saw two texts from my ex: "I think it's official now" with a screenshot of the court entry that read "2026-03-20 JUDGEMENT, DISSOLUTION OF MARRIAGE, MARITAL STATUS ENDS MAR-20-2026".

March 20 was yesterday, I went to SF city hall for the second time in my life. The first was for my own marriage ceremony where my parents who live in China and don't speak any English surprise visited me to join the ceremony. Yesterday, I was there to witness a dear friend's marriage, and my divorce paper was finally approved on that same day, after having been submitted over half a year ago. Life comes full circle.

I'm officially divorced now.

I had many ideas about how I was going to feel when this day comes, which is almost exactly two years after I had broken up and moved out. I thought I was going to throw a big party and proudly announce to all my friends, that I was going to feel so relieved. Instead I was holding back tears while trying to make it through the class, and they weren't exactly happy tears, and I've been trying to figure out what they are.

I responded back to him with some logistics questions, and eventually asked, how have you been. He left me on read. I realized he still resents me, for taking some of his money, not even all that I was entitled to, because he still thought I shouldn't have taken any. I realized now that we are officially divorced, I probably will never hear from him again. Our closure ends at the divorce paper that will come into the mail.

TWO DAYS AGO, I told my manager that I'm going on medical leave, after having another meltdown at midnight the night before, which kept me up most of the night. My therapist was only comfortable writing me on leave for three weeks, which made me anxious, because I very well know that three weeks is not enough.

I've always been anxious, but the anxiety level since joining Uber has been steadily rising. I had a wakeup call when my bodyworker told me that it feels like my body has been in chronic stress that he's not able to access anything. I went back to therapy, and had to go through a whole search to find a new one because my old one doesn't take Uber's insurance, but it felt too late.

The details of what happened at work is not even worth going into, but it was something like, my team set up was super tough - team scope and direction was changing constantly, people refused to work together, and after a year of trying to make it work, I was exhausted and ready to switch teams for a change. I interviewed and got some offers, my team found out and talked me out by reassuring me how valuable I was to the team and giving me resources on a big project that I was leading that finally showed promising results. I stayed, only to be taken off the big project that I started, three weeks after I turned down the internal offers. That was when I realized my manager does not care about my feelings and development, so I re-engaged with the other teams. It was too close to performance review cycle that I had to wait for my rating before the transfer, then two weeks later I got a below average rating, making any internal transfer very difficult. I know I can still network around and there are people that will likely take me, but after all of this following an exhausting year, I had nothing left in me. I sank into self doubts on my competency as a PM, my judgements on who I can trust vs not, what I still could have done but didn't because I was scared, ashamed, or just too exhausted.

I have had so little energy towards things I used to love this year, I've fallen out of touch with friends because I don't want to bitch about work anymore, but that's also the only thing top of mind for me. I couldn't fall asleep because I was anxious, then I was anxious about not being able to sleep, I eventually do and all I dream about is work, and I wake up feeling so hopeless. More days than not, I took sleeping pills to sleep.

For the first time, I had a taste of depression, and I felt closer to my mom. I thought of her a lot, my last and few memories of her before she left us forever. I wondered if this is my trigger to a long episode of depression, like giving birth to me was her trigger to her decline.

I learned about the option of a medical leave - I reached out to a few friends that have done it, they all encouraged me to take the time and put myself first. I reached out to my two team members who did it, they both got fired soon after returning from leave (even though it's technically illegal), but they don't regret that at all.

I imagined how my manager and my teammates would think of me. I asked myself if I'm prepared to be fired after coming back from leave. I thought of how tough the job market is, and if not being able to find another job I like for a while will hurt my self esteem even more, and make me even more depressed...

Then on Wednesday, I was having dinner with Andrew and two of his OG Uber friends. I briefly shared my experience with one of them while waiting for the group to arrive, and he asked did you try to ask to this leader what happened, if they liked you, they would probably support you. I sank into sadness throughout the whole night, and had a breakdown when I got home. Another wave of self blaming, why didn't I do that, why am I so weak, how am I ever going to survive any corporate world, how come everyone else has got it figured out and I couldn't.

I decided then that I needed to go on leave. Andrew held me tight and said that he was fully supportive.

So the next day, I told my manager. It's been hard to read her, and I no longer trust her, or my judgements of her, but since it's only for three weeks first, she didn't react too much. I know I shouldn't even care about her reactions, but I do.

So today, Mar 21 2026, I became a free woman, free from a marriage where two people who deeply cared for each other grew apart, free from a toxic environment that made me doubt and blame myself.

I hope I can finally go look for my purpose in life. Not as a partner. Not as a worker. As me.
