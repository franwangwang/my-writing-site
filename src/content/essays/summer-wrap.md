Sitting outside of Maison Nico on a Friday at 11am, Transamerica building behind my back where I spent 12 hours everyday during my first year in SF - a full circle moment for me. Journey entries have become sparse since I made the big decision in March, but A LOT has happened since then. Since I have a few hours for myself today, I figured I'll note down a few things that's been on my mind lately, and also, to catch you up.

## KPIs

I forgot who told me they kept a tracker for dates they went on, I heard it and immediately knew I wanted to do it too - call me psycho, but it felt like progress, that I'm putting myself out there, that I'm trying and working towards something. So I had this Google Spreadsheet titled "a year of explorations" where each row represented one person that I went on a date with, followed by the date(s) that we went out, the venue(s) we met at, and some brief notes on the person. They would read like this:

B, 5/1, 5/5, Sip & Guzzle, fk boi - what i wanted in nyc

JE, 6/2, many mutual friends and connections, said she was autistic, convo became really good towards the end, she's big into directing and working on a documentary, would love to hang more

JN, 6/21, no chemistry, texted too much and talked a lot. Tipped $2 for our $36 bill and touched my legs

I told Peter that my KPI for NYC in April was to go on one date. I achieved by 300% and more, went on five dates with three different people, 1 guy 2 girls, and slept with the guy.

When I settled down in SF, I made dating my mission. I even made a calendar and marked the days that I went on a date, color coded by first or second+ dates. My tracker would tell me that the month following May 28 (my first date in SF, also the most significant that he deserves a whole section), I met 11 people, 9 guys 2 women, 17 dates in total.

Then by July 1, I threw myself in recruiting. I also had a tracker, and a calendar, color coded by which round it was (recruiter vs. hiring manager vs. later stage, etc.) It made me feel productive to see that my recruiting calendar looked busier than "a year of explorations". I canceled hangouts, dates, pulled back on work, and had my first offer on 8/12. I think I overdid it a bit, that by the time I got my first offer, I felt too burned out to continue pushing forward the other processes. So that's that.

## Chan

Yeah Chan would probably deserve his own section. He was the first guy that I matched on Hinge with in SF. His profile had a choice of what we could do on our first date, I selected "co-create a piece of art". We exchanged a few back and forth on what to do, next thing I know I was in his apartment trying to carve an ice sculpture, which wow was really really hard. Chan is obsessed with in person experience, he hosted a few drinks nights, where he would make 3 drinks + snacks pairings for 8 people at a time, but since a lot of people don't drink in SF nowadays, it would be 6 drinks, 3 alcoholic and 3 non alcoholic. It would all be his own creation, and he posts the recipes on his fridge. He made me a couple drinks, they were all very delicious. On our last date, we would also milk wash a couple drinks together because I told him I wanted to learn - milk wash still has a soft spot in my heart.

Chan is the kind of guy that I would love to travel with. I was most attracted to his "it's either a good idea or a good story" mentality, and his artistic side, let it be writing, or his memory project, or him as a dancer. He posts his writings on his blog, the one that caught my eyes the most was "Three Dates" where he talked about his three dates in one night in Shanghai when he lost his voice so was completely silent. I definitely fell in love with his writing.

He had these AI-generated paintings on his walls. Turns out each of them represented a core memory of his. I asked him about each one of them - most of them evolved around crazy Tinder date stories. I later realized that I felt the most connected when he was sharing these stories. What does that say about me? Probably the kind of things that I would love to do, but not brave enough, because I haven't been single during my adult life until now, because I'm a girl and need to be more careful with my safety, because I'm shy physically and scared to initiate even though I'm also always down.

Chan and I had some pretty magical dates. Second date we met at Barry's, and I pushed myself to sprint at 12 miles/hr in the end. Then we went to Moongate in Chinatown, the setup felt a bit like a theatre, our seats were against the walls and we were both facing the center of the space, speculating if other pairs are going on first dates. I told him then that I'm married but separated. His questions were if we are on good terms and if I'm really ready to date again. We ate some bar food and then went back to his place to drink some spinach smoothies since we both felt that we needed veggies after the fried food. Third date we left SF at 9am to go hiking in Mt. Tam, the trail turned out to be such a gem and ended on the beach. We laid on a rock for a while and watched the seagulls and listened to the waves. It was one of the few very warm days in SF, we got back to the city and grabbed sandos at Palm City and went to lay on North Beach. It was super sandy, so we went back to his place, and he suggested taking a shower together. He washed me up carefully and I did the same. He had just adopted the kitty Stan, he would stand by the showers to check us out and eventually lose interest. Then we had sex and it was pretty good for our first time. By the time he drove me home, it was 6:30pm and I was pretty late for my roommate's birthday party.

## Connections

I crave connections, I always knew that. That makes saying goodbye, especially ones that I didn't expect/want to accept, so so hard. Chan was one, I would ruminate over what I should and should not have said and done for weeks and weeks after he ended things.

I realized that this was particularly hard for me in the dating app world. If the date went well, it means I felt a strong connection. When things ultimately don't work out romantically as vast majority of dating app relationships tend to, people typically will just stop meeting all together. No one seems to be looking for friends. Except for me.

I told WY this. My notes on WY after our first date was "led a voice part at the a cappella drop in; parents from Taiwan, super smart, very well mannered, asked thoughtful questions." Our second date was going well, then I said I'm looking for someone who can explore the world with me, and he said then I don't think I'm the guy for you. I'm not at all surprised, we connect on a lot of things and he's very active in different communities in SF, but he's definitely a bit too home bodied for me. I was still a bit down, however, after we said goodbye. Until he actually hit me up a couple weeks later inviting me to an event at the Commons, and then again at a board game that he was organizing. I guess I felt that a connection was regained after I thought I had lost it.

The Commons is an interesting place. An is against it because it's a paid membership based community, and he hates concepts where people "pay to play". Chan goes to the Sunday Writing Club there. WY is a member, so is WY's friend Jinny, who he introduced me to and I felt very connected with. JR, the 25 year old girl that I went on a date with, is also a member and leads the artist group there. WY first brought me there for a casual hangout event, where I had very deep discussion with a random group. I later went there by myself for a music jam session. I was definitely one with the least amount of musical experience and I don't play any instruments, but that was the most present and connected I had felt for a while. I particularly bonded with a Chinese girl who wrote a song about a date she had, how they had crazy connections but he doesn't live here and needed to catch a flight out of the city that night. I caught up on the melody within a couple measures. People were surprised to learn that I'd never done any improv/music jam and said that I harmonized perfectly. That inspired me to sign up for voice lessons - first one in an hour.

## San Francisco

This summer made me fall in love with SF. For the sunset runs in Golden Gate Park; for the random invites to events and everyone welcoming me; for all the interesting people I met on dates who are writers, dancers, musicians, movie directors, standup comedians, triathlon crushers, circus performers, bike mechanics, community builders; for making me feel young and energized being 31 and single, for making me feel like I have a full life ahead of me.
